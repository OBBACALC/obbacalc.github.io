Pennsylvania OBBBA — FAQs

Does Pennsylvania conform to the OBBBA overtime deduction?

Pennsylvania has not yet made a final decision. As a static conformity state, it must pass legislation. A decision is expected before April 15, 2026.

Can I claim both the overtime AND tips deduction as a Pennsylvania resident?

Yes — both deductions can be claimed on the same return. The overtime deduction (max $12,500 single / $25,000 MFJ) and tips deduction (max $25,000) are calculated separately. Phase-out begins at $150,000 MAGI (single) or $300,000 MAGI (MFJ), reducing by $100 per $1,000 above the threshold. Both are claimed on Schedule 1-A of Form 1040.

When is the filing deadline for Pennsylvania residents?

The federal and Pennsylvania state tax filing deadline is April 15, 2026 for tax year 2025. File on time to claim your OBBBA deductions — extensions give more time to file, not more time to pay.

Does the OBBBA deduction reduce Social Security taxes?

No. The deduction only reduces your federal (and state, if applicable) income tax. FICA taxes — Social Security at 6.2% and Medicare at 1.45% — are still owed on all wages including overtime and tips.
